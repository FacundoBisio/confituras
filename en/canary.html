A pedir de boca – Tropical Fruits. Canary Islands

13 Sep 2011

Documentary about tropical fruits grown and consumed in the Canary Islands: papaya , mango, starfruit, caimito, red mamey, bananas, and avocado. It explains cultivation, plant and fruit characteristics, medicinal properties, how each fruit is presented, and tasting recipes.

In Spain, the Huerta de Murcia is traditionally known as a benchmark for fruit and vegetable cultivation. The Canary Islands are mainly known for banana production. However, less known to many, the Canary Islands produce some of the best tropical and exotic fruit crops nationally. This is mainly due to: (1) sunny, tropical weather during most of the year, and (2) a maritime climate influenced by the Atlantic Ocean. These conditions create fruit with great aroma and intensity.

Canary Islands: The Garden of the Atlantic

A unique climate, volcanic soil, and ocean breezes create the perfect conditions for the world's most exquisite tropical fruits.

Why are Canary fruits unique?

The Canary Islands enjoy a privileged position. The permanent sunshine and mild temperatures year-round allow fruits to ripen on the tree, concentrating their natural sugars and aroma. Unlike imported fruits that ripen in chambers, our raw materials are harvested at their optimal point.

Our Tropical Treasures

• Papaya: Known as the "fruit of good health." In the Canaries, we cultivate sweet and buttery varieties that are the foundation of our most popular jams. Rich in antioxidants and vitamin C.
• Mango: The king of tropical fruits finds its ideal home on our shores. Providing a smooth texture and intense flavor that captures the essence of the tropics.
• Guava: A vitamin C explosion (even more than oranges). Its distinctive and aromatic flavor is a classic of Canary pastry-making.

Climate, soil, and tradition

Volcanic soils and Atlantic breezes create fruits with intense aroma and natural sweetness. This is the base of our artisan process: harvest at the right moment, cook slowly, and preserve the original character of each fruit.

From papaya to guava, every jar is a small piece of the islands.
