The Best Canary Islands Jams: A Guide for Discerning Palates and Professionals

When seeking the essence of the Canary Islands, the flavour of its tropical fruits is the true protagonist. Canary jams have evolved from a traditional product to a benchmark of premium gastronomy, especially appreciated by hotels, restaurants and gourmet shops.

What distinguishes an authentic artisan Canary jam?

Not all preserves meet the quality standard required by a five-star breakfast. To recognise the best artisan jams from Tenerife and the archipelago, three keys must be considered:

  • Origin of raw materials: km 0 fruits such as papaya, mango, guava or Canary banana.
  • Traditional cooking technique: recipes inherited from the 1960s that prioritise the natural concentration of the fruit.
  • No additives: a quality extra jam does not need artificial colourants or preservatives.

Essential varieties

  • Papaya and Orange Jam: perfect balance between tropical sweetness and citrus acidity.
  • Mango Preserve: ideal for pairing with Canary cheeses.
  • Guava Jam: essential in hotel buffets.
  • Sugar-free options: a growing trend in healthy gastronomy.

The legacy: Palmelita Jams

Within this landscape, Palmelita Jams stands out. Founded in 1968 by Werner Bittermann in Bajamar, it has set the standard for what a premium jam means. Its philosophy combines Central European tradition and the richness of Tenerife's tropical fruits.

Choosing a supplier that maintains artisan processes guarantees a superior product and a history of quality that customers value.

Where to find professional quality jams?

The best option is to go directly to traditional workshops or contact historic producers like Palmelita. This ensures freshness, traceability and support for the local Canary economy.
