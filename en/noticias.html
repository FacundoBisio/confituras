Palmelita selected as a local product for Madrid Fusión 2023

Diario de Avisos
Wednesday, January 18, 2023

Confituras Palmelita was chosen to represent Candelaria’s local products at Madrid Fusión 2023, one of the most important gastronomy events in Spain. The municipality’s participation was part of the “Local Product Club”, an initiative that promotes the value of regional cuisine and local producers.

This was Candelaria’s first time taking part in Madrid Fusión, held from January 23 to 25, 2023 at the Cabildo de Tenerife stand. The goal was to strengthen Candelaria’s position as a gastronomic and sustainable destination, showcasing quality, tradition, and proximity-grown products.

  • Institutional representation: led by the Candelaria City Council alongside chefs and producers.
  • Focus: highlighting local products and traditional Canarian cuisine.
  • Context: the Local Product Club has operated since 2020 promoting agricultural, commercial, and economic development.
  • Presence: Palmelita was featured alongside other key gastronomic representatives of the municipality.

“From Bajamar to the heart of Europe”

Diario de Avisos
Sunday, June 21, 2015

Palmelita took a major step in its international expansion, bringing its artisanal jams from its workshop in Bajamar to the German market— competing with established European brands through a premium identity rooted in origin and traditional craftsmanship.

Founded in 1968 as a family business, Palmelita grew beyond the Canary Islands after strong acceptance in supermarkets and specialty stores. By 2014, it had expanded across the islands and key Spanish cities; by mid-June 2015, its products began reaching important cities in Germany.

  • Product style: artisan jams made with seasonal Canary fruit and vegetables.
  • Unique value: produce sourced directly from local farmers at peak ripeness.
  • Profile: premium product free of preservatives and artificial coloring—focused on natural aroma and intensity.
  • Prior distribution: presence in chains such as Spar, HiperDino, El Corte Inglés, and Supercor.

“Palmelita begins exporting its jams to Germany”

La Opinión de Tenerife
Friday, June 19, 2015

In 2015, Palmelita officially began exporting its jams to Germany, marking a significant milestone for a brand built on artisanal confectionery tradition.

Confituras Palmelita was founded in 1968 by master pastry chef Werner Bittermann (1928–2014) in a small workshop. Decades later, thanks to its traditional methods and the quality of seasonal Canary fruit, the brand reached new European markets—especially Germany.

  • Foundation year: 1968, rooted in traditional confectionery.
  • Export milestone: first shipments to Germany in mid-2015.
  • Positioning: artisan “Made in the Canary Islands” premium brand.
  • Narrative: a family legacy evolving into a modern export-ready company.
