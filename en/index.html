The Best Canary Islands Jams: Guide for Discerning Palates and Professionals

When seeking the essence of the Canary Islands, the flavour of its tropical fruits is the true protagonist. Canary jams have evolved from a traditional product to a benchmark of premium gastronomy, especially appreciated by hotels, restaurants, and gourmet shops.

What sets an authentic artisanal Canary jam apart?

Not all preserves meet the quality standard demanded by a five-star breakfast. To recognise the best artisanal jams from Tenerife and the archipelago, three key factors must be considered:

  • Origin of raw materials: locally-sourced fruits such as papaya, mango, guava or Canary bananas.
  • Traditional cooking technique: recipes inherited from the 1960s that prioritise the natural concentration of fruit.
  • No additives: an extra-quality preserve needs no artificial colourings or preservatives.

Essential varieties

  • Papaya and Orange Jam: perfect balance between tropical sweetness and citrus acidity.
  • Mango Preserve: ideal for pairing with Canary cheeses.
  • Guava Jam: essential in hotel buffets.
  • Sugar-free options: a growing trend in healthy gastronomy.

The legacy: Palmelita Preserves

Within this landscape, Palmelita Preserves stands out. Founded in 1968 by Werner Bittermann in Bajamar, it has set the standard for what a premium jam means. Its philosophy combines Central European tradition and the richness of Tenerife's tropical fruits.

Choosing a supplier that maintains artisanal processes guarantees a superior product and a story of quality that customers value.

Where to find professional-quality jams?

The best option is to go directly to traditional workshops or contact historic producers such as Palmelita. This ensures freshness, traceability, and support for the local Canary economy.
